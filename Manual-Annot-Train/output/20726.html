Alignment score (AS): reflects how many mismatches and gaps you need to align the read at a particular position. Mapping quality score (MAPQ): reflects the probability that the read was wrongly mapped, i.e. not aligned where it should. Usually reported on a PHRED scale. When mapping reads, it is the mapping quality score that matters, as it is essential to know where to map the read. Poor alignment scores are acceptable, since they may convey important information, like the presence of several mutations, or simply the fact that the strain is more distantly related to the reference that was used for mapping.
Alignment score versus mapping quality score.
Alignment score Mapping quality score Interpretation
HIGH HIGH Read maps unambiguously and is very similar to reference sequence at that position
HIGH LOW Read is very similar to reference sequence at that position, but maps at several positions
LOW HIGH Read maps unambiguously, but aligns with several mismatches to reference sequence at that position
LOW LOW Reads aligns with several mismatches at this and several other positions in the reference sequences
