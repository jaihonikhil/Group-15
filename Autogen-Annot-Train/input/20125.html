Metastasis and prostate cancer–related death were overall rare events, but their rates were higher in the Gleason 3 + 4 and/or “MRI-visible” disease groups. Treatment rates were higher in patients with an MRI lesion at baseline. Most treated patients underwent focal therapy, predominantly high-intensity focused ultrasound. Out of 21 men who transitioned to WW, only one was previously upgraded to Gleason ≥4 + 3, whereas the rest transitioned to WW due to comorbidities, advanced age, or personal preference.
