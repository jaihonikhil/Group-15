In general, when light propagates towards the surface of a material, part of the incoming radiation is transmitted and part of the incoming radiation is reflected at the boundary between the two materials. The transmitted amplitude, t , and the reflected amplitude, r , are given by Fresnel's equations [ , ] and depend on the angle of incidence, θ 0 , the angle of refraction, θ , and the polarization of the light.
