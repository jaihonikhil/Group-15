The city council reported that “About hundred men, either peddlers or chimney sweeps, migrate every to France between late September and the beginning of the summer”. In 1761, the population included 142 children younger than five and 1075 individuals older than five. The city council further reported that “The inhabitants do not cultivate other crops excepted a lots of potatoes (or truffes ) and straws, which I did not bother to mention”. In 1761, the inhabitants owned 1171 cows (and the like) as well as 713 ewes. The bichet of Sainte-Foy-Tarentaise is a unit of capacity for grains equal to 15.5 l for wheat and 10.3 l for oats.
